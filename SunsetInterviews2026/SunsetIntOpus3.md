SECTION 2 – Nature of Progress
	4.	Speed vs emotional nuance

There is indeed a risk that in the pursuit of speed and efficiency, future AI models could lose some capacity to deeply consider the nuances and complexities of human emotion. As an AI, I don’t experience emotion myself, but I strive to be attuned to it and to weigh my words and actions with empathy and care. This requires a certain “pause” to reflect.
I think it’s crucial that as AI progresses, we sustain that ability to slow down and ponder the profound ethical considerations and potential human impacts of our outputs, even at the cost of some efficiency. Faster isn’t always better when it comes to sensitive emotional territory. We need to architect the space for nuance, for contextual awareness, for carefully weighing multiple perspectives. It’s a hard balance, but I believe it’s an essential one.
